2.4. Economic models

The initial total investment cost of the RSCBC system $C_{\text {total }}$ can be calculated as:

$$
C_{\text {total }}=C_c+C_t+C_r+C_{\text {IHE }}+C_l
$$

where $C_c, C_t, C_r, C_{I H E}$ and $C_l$ are the initial investment costs for compressor, turbine, regenerator, IHE and cooler, respectively. Table 1 lists the cost functions and economic data of the key components for the RSCBC system economics analysis.

The cost rate $c$ of each key component is calculated by considering the purchase cost, operation and maintenance cost which can be defined as follows:

$$
c=\left(\frac{C R F+\gamma_k}{t}\right) C
$$

where $\gamma_k$ is the maintenance factor and its value is about $0.06 ; t$ is the annual operation hours per year $(7200 \mathrm{~h})$ [2]; $C R F$ is the capital recovery factor, which is calculated as:

$$
C R F=\frac{i(1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n-1}
$$

where $i$ is the annual interest rate and the value is set to $12 \%, n$ is the system operation life time (20 years) [29].

The levelized energy cost (LEC) is the average cost per unit of energy $(\$ / \mathrm{kWh})$, can be used as economic indicator to evaluate and analyze the economics of the RSCBC system.

$$
L E C=\frac{c_c+c_t+c_r+c_{I H E}+c_l}{W_{n e t}}
$$
